Lailatul Qadr: The Night of Power

Lailatul Qadr, known as the Night of Power, is one of the most sacred nights in Islam. It is believed to be the night when the Holy Quran was first revealed to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) through Angel Jibreel (Gabriel). This night holds immense spiritual significance and is described in the Quran as being better than a thousand months.

The Revelation and Significance

According to Islamic tradition, Lailatul Qadr falls within the last ten nights of Ramadan, specifically on one of the odd-numbered nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th). Though the exact date is unknown, the 27th night of Ramadan is most commonly observed as Lailatul Qadr.

Indeed, We sent the Quran down during the Night of Decree. And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree? The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months. The angels and the Spirit (Jibreel) descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter. Peace it is until the emergence of dawn. (Quran 97:1-5)

A Story from the Time of the Prophet

One day, Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) shared with his companions a story of a righteous man from the past who worshipped Allah for 1,000 months (83 years and 4 months) without rest. The companions were astonished and wished they could achieve such devotion. In response, Allah revealed Surah Al-Qadr, telling them that worshipping on Lailatul Qadr is equivalent to worshipping for a thousand months.

What to Do on Lailatul Qadr?

  • Praying Tahajjud (Night Prayers): Engaging in extra prayers and seeking forgiveness.
  • Reciting the Quran: Reading and reflecting upon the words of Allah.
  • Making Du’a (Supplication): Asking Allah for mercy, guidance, and forgiveness.
  • Seeking Forgiveness: The Prophet (PBUH) taught a special dua to say on this night:

Allahumma innaka ‘afuwwun tuhibbul ‘afwa fa’fu ‘anni (O Allah, You are Most Forgiving, and You love to forgive, so forgive me.)

  • Giving Charity: Helping those in need brings great rewards, especially on this night.

A Night of Transformation

Many Muslims have experienced life-changing moments on Lailatul Qadr. It is said that those who spend the night in sincere worship, with a pure heart and deep devotion, will have their past sins forgiven and their prayers answered. The spiritual energy and peace that descend on this night leave a lasting impact on the hearts of believers.

May we all be blessed to witness Lailatul Qadr and receive its countless blessings. Ameen.
